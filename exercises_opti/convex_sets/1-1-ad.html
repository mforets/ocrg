Let \(S = \left\{ a \in \mathbb{R}^k \text{ s.t. } ~p_a(t) \in \mathbb{R}[t] ~\mathrm{satisfies} ~p_a(0)=1 ~\text{and}~ |p_a(t)| \leq 1 ~\text{for}~ t\in [\alpha,\beta]\right\}\), where \(p_a(t) := a_1+a_2 t + \ldots + a_k t^{k-1}\).

Is \(S\) convex?

Solution (MF). Consider two points \(a, a' \in S \subset \mathbb{R}^k\), and arbitrary \(\theta \in [0,1]\). Consider the intermediate point \(a_\theta := (1-\theta)a + \theta a'\). Polynomials are linear in their coefficients; explicitly, \[ \begin{align} p_{a_\theta}(t) &= \left( (1-\theta)a_1 + \theta a'_1 \right) + \left( (1-\theta)a_1 + \theta a'_1 \right)t + \ldots + \left( (1-\theta)a_1 + \theta a'_1 \right) t^{k-1} \\ &= (a_1 + a_2 t + \ldots a_k t_{k-1})(1-\theta) + (a'_1 + a'_2 t + \ldots a'_k t_{k-1})\theta \\ &= p_a(t) (1-\theta) + p_{a'}(t)\theta. \end{align} \] It is easy to check that \(p_{a_\theta}(t)\) satisfies \(p_{a_\theta}(0)=1\) and \[ |p_{a_\theta}(t)| \leq |p_a(t)| (1-\theta) + |p_{a'}(t)|\theta \leq 1. \] Hence, \(a_\theta \in S\) and \(S\) is convex. \(\square\)
